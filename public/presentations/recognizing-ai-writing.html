Recognizing AI Writing

Stephen Zweibel

Digital Scholarship Librarian, Graduate Center, CUNY

AI detectors don't work very well

  • False positives are common, including on real student writing
  • Anyone who edits AI output a little can defeat them
  • Pangram and the others give you a probability score, not a verdict
  • I find detection a losing place to start

Mostly, it's exposure

  • Read enough AI writing and you start to see it
  • No single tell does the work
  • Here are a few things to look for

Read for the template

  • Ask what rhetorical pattern the text is running
  • Each paragraph in AI prose tends to do a specific rhetorical job
  • The opener often announces what that job is
  • The reader has something to point at

The first-sentence scan

  • Read only the first sentence of each paragraph before reading for content
  • If those openers form a neat rhetorical ladder, the writing may be running an AI template
  • It's the fastest method I know that doesn't need a tool

Lowercase and “lowkey”

  • People humanize AI output with lowercase, sprinkled “lowkey,” broken fragments
  • Run the first-sentence scan anyway

Hachette's Shy Girl (March 2026)

Mia Ballard's horror novel, canceled in the U.S. and discontinued in the U.K. after readers flagged suspected AI prose. A passage from the book:

My breath evens out, the outline of possibility taking shape. A job. The thought lingers, solid and improbable. A flicker of hope. When the call ends, I sit for a moment, the phone warm in my hand, the silence loud. That fragile swell of hope trying to push through the cracks. But the feeling doesn't last. The buzz of a notification snaps me back, its vibration rippling through the table like an electric jolt. Nathan's name flashes on the screen. My chest tightens, anticipation blooming sharp and fast, and I unlock the phone with a clumsiness that betrays me.

The Slate review flagged repeated parallel constructions and excessive simile use. Ballard says a freelance editor added AI-generated elements without her knowledge.

Pangram's cafe-review contrast

Two reviews of Textbook Cafe in Brooklyn. Same place, same prompt.

AI

Textbook Cafe is a delightful gem! This cozy spot offers an inviting ambiance perfect for anyone looking to enjoy a quiet afternoon. The sandwiches here are simply superb, with fresh ingredients and imaginative combinations that satisfy both traditional and adventurous palates. The service is friendly and attentive, ensuring a pleasant experience every time. Whether you're stopping by for a quick lunch or settling in with a good book, Textbook Cafe is the perfect place to unwind and treat yourself to some delicious fare.

HUMAN

We split a merguez breakfast sandwich, a fish sandwich, and some fries. The breakfast sandwich was solid though I think it could have been even better if the merguez patty was broken up into little chunks and cooked with the eggs in sort of a scramble to better distribute it. Some bites were just sausage and nothing else. The fish sando was pretty good and compared to many similar sandwiches, it was huge. Almost too much bread? But that's a better problem than a puny sandwich. The fries had this Tunisian seasoning that didn't really do it for me. It smelled good but tasted a little bitter.

Authorship is a spectrum

Pangram now describes four points along a spectrum.

  • Fully human-written
  • Lightly AI-assisted
  • Moderately AI-assisted
  • Fully AI-generated

Many of the cases readers flag fall in the middle two. Disclosure policy hasn't caught up to that yet.

Exercise

Hand out three short pieces with the sources hidden:

  • An unedited AI response
  • An AI response edited for tone but not structure
  • A human-written piece with its own unevenness and local specificity

Ask people to mark:

  • Paragraph openers that announce the paragraph's job
  • Phrases that announce significance without backing it up
  • Places where a concrete detail would sharpen the prose

The goal

  • Read more carefully
  • Revise more carefully
  • Ask better questions about authorship and disclosure
  • Catching cheaters is a different problem, and the detectors don't work anyway
